Why software engineering

I studied chemical engineering at Carnegie Mellon University. After graduating college, I worked as an environmental engineering consultant where many of my projects involved analyzing client data using Microsoft Excel. Although I enjoyed helping my clients, the tools I used were often the cause for the workflow bottleneck such that they would crash during an analysis. This made me to want to learn about how applications are built and how to fix this problem. As data analysis is used by a wide range of people, from a small business to a large company, I wanted to create something that would have an impact.
